# 4Funner Vibes website 4funnervibes.com
#### Video Demo:  <https://youtu.be/BYWb-f95KQg>
#### Description:
My CS50 final project was building a website for documenting my wife's and my travels along the Pan American Highway.
I originally took CS50 as the first step on my journey to become a web developer and am happy to report that I found 
this project to be a lot of fun! I learned a bunch more about using Bootstrap and found another CSS library, Tailwind, 
that I want to try out for future projects. This project also prompted me to create a Google Sheet for tracking my hours 
on projects (I even built in efficiency so I don't charge future clients as much for unproductive periods) and I have to 
admit I did geek out quite a bit over that.

For the design of the websites I took a look at other overlanders' sites to get an idea of what I wanted it to look 
like. The people whose sites I looked at were Desk to Glory, The Mortells, Dirt Sunrise and The Dangerz. For future 
potential upgrades I might want to indlude some type of parallax scrolling as well as a page with a clickable interactive 
map showing the different countries we've been to and giving a bit of our thoughs on the areas.

#### What the different files do
##### index.html
This is the main landing page for the website. It contains a small blurb about us and another small blurb about our travel 
highlights so far. I really liked the way The Mortells website had an image with a menu bar and then the user scrolls down 
to view the content so borrowed that; it took me a surprising length ot time to figure out that I needed a "container-fluid" 
instead of just a regular "container" for the main div.

##### highlights.html
This is the page where we talk about some of the highlights on our trip so far. This will probably be the page we look back on the most later in life when we want to remember the trip. After a bunch of searching on the bootstrap website I found "col-sm" for 
stacking objects horizontally on a computer and vertically on mobile.

##### rig.html
This page talks about our vehicle, which is almost just as much a part of the team as the people on any overland trip. This was probably the simplest page to implement; the only thing fun and exciting is a mailto link and a link to Overland Kitchen.

##### us.html
This is where we talk more in depth about each of us individually and as a team. We also included a link to send us an email if people are interested in doing that. I didn't think that writing about ourselves would be fun but it actually was!
