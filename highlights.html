Travel Highlights

Spending a month in Alaska

We were super fortunate to get to spend a full month in the beautiful state of Alaska! Driving around there really gave us an appreciation for how truly massive it is. We will definitely be back!

Seeing Denali

Thus far this is the highlight of the trip for Andy. Its massiveness is something that really has to be seen in person to be felt; words and photos can only try to do it justice.

The Grewingk Glacier hike

This is Nadeesha's trip highlight so far. It's a fun day hike to Grewingk Glacier which included a boat ride to and from the trailhead. Mako Water Taxi even threw in free beers on the ride back! Check out our video summary!

Seeing a grizzly bear

We saw plenty of black bears in Canada and Alaska but hadn't seen a single grizzly until we spotted this little guy fishing in Valdez! Our friends Marcus and Bianca of Not Another Campervan saw their first grizzly of the trip on the same day!

Getting to meet and spend time with fellow travelers

Meeting and hanging out with new people has been great! It's been far easier to meet and make friends than we would've thought.

Jasper National Park

We got to Jasper the first day that the campgrounds in the park were opened back up after a wildfire in the area. It was pretty cool to be there with just ourselves, the wildlife, and a few other spontaneous guests!

The scenery and wildlife north of Atigun Pass on the Dalton Highway

Driving the Dalton (or haul road as it's sometimes called) was a pretty cool experience and the scenery got especially beautiful once we were north of Atigun Pass. We saw several Caribou and even some Musk Oxen!

Getting to see both Tuktoyaktuk and Prudhoe Bay

When we set out we still weren't sure if we were going to get to the Arctic Ocean at Tuktoyaktuk or at Prudhoe Bay. Prudhoe Bay is further north but you aren't able to drive your own vehicle to the ocean because it's a government owned oilfield and you have to take a bus. After talking it over with our friends Chris and Al while sipping whiskey at Northwest Overland Rally we decided to take Al's suggestion and just do both!

Making it to the Arctic Ocean

What a cool feeling! By the time we got to the Arctic Ocean in Tuktoyaktuk we had put on just over 10,000 miles after leaving Minnesota. It was pretty cool to actually get to set foot in the ocean. We even met a nice local lady who told us a bit about the history of Tuktoyaktuk and the people who call it home.
